Thesis

Work In Progress

An exploration to see if it possible to create rich educational experiences that are based on traditional forms of Indian storytelling.

This thesis project is strongly inspired by Kavaad which is a rich form of storytelling that originates in Rajasthan, India. It is a box structure that opens to reveal pictures of episodes in a particular story, or more frequently in a series of stories about a particular character

My thesis revolves around the idea of combining this with todayâ€™s education. For instance, use this form and structure to say a story about physics, electronics etc.

Why is this needed ?

  • To create an engaging learning tool that moves away from the digital and towards the tangiable
  • To encourage learning through puzzles and games
  • (In the long run) Make this a low cost and open source tool that can be easily used by teachers and parents

Audience

This is primarily meant for kids who are beginning to learn electronics/physics (between the age of 6 - 10). Another audience this would be useful to is adults who are interested in learning new concepts in a fun and engaging way.
