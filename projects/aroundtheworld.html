Around the World

Accessibility, Programming


Concept design of a phone app that gamifies physiotheraphy and keeps track of patients progress.

The Question

How can you ensure that patients continue with their physiotherapy without having to go to the hospital/clinic for each session ?

The problems with physiotheraphy(PT) are the following -

  • PT can be stressful and even painful
  • There in a lack of motivation/delight in doing PT
  • Most people forget to do PT regularly

Takeaway from User testing

Through user testing with people from the age range of 21-65, below were the takeaways

  • Process itself was very dry and boring ( and painful )
  • While reminders can be annoying they can be helpful in this case
  • Even after the immediate pain goes away, patients need to be reminded to continue their PT

The Solution

Based on the idea of travelling the world ( in 80 days ), this phone app - Will help and remind patients to complete PT Tries to gamify the experience Could also help keep track of patient's progress and trigger reminders if needed

Doctors and patients together form the plan

The plan contains the list of exercises and the number of days to do them.

Patient then gets a “travel plan” Each day of exercise helps them progress and go to the next location

Every day they start their exercise in a new location User straps phone onto leg and app tracks basic motion Alerts you after duration of exercise Can do exercises in parts - it will remind you at regular intervals through the day Plays music/videos to help take away some boredom

On completion of the days exercise you move on to the next city.
